Delmar Larsen
LibreTexts Director Professor Department of Chemistry University of California, Davis
Delmar Larsen is a Professor in the Department of Chemistry and the Biophysics Graduate Group in the University of California, Davis. Delmar received his Ph.D. from the University of Chicago (Chemistry) and did postdoctoral stints at the Free University Amsterdam (Biophysics) and the University of Southern California (Chemistry). In 2005, Delmar moved to the UC Davis as an Assistant Professor, was promoted to Associate Professor in 2012, and was promoted to Full Professor in 2019. Delmar‚Äôs current research interests extends across many scientific disciplines including biophysics, physical chemistry, molecular biology, and computational modeling, with a common thread of investigating and characterizing of rapid condensed phase dynamics. Delmar is the Founder and Director of the LibreTexts project consisting of twelve independently operating and interconnected libraries that focus on augmenting post-secondary education in specific fields in both STEM fields, social sciences, and humanities. Supported by a U.S. National Science Foundation ($1 million grants), the LibreTexts project is being built by over 1000 active developers (students, instructors and outside experts) across multiple campuses and nations. The chemistry library is the most trafficked chemistry education site in the world and the entire project is responsible for over 50 million student visitors annually. LibreTexts has served students with over 220 million pages since 2008, which resulted in half a millennium of confirmed reading. Approximately $US 30 million have been saved the past two years in the postsecondary textbook costs of over 40 campuses; much more is expected to be save in the future as the project grows is still growing exponentially in traffic and use.

Robert Belford
Developer Professor Department of Chemistry University of Arkansas at Little Rock
Bob Belford is a Professor of Chemistry at the University of Arkansas Little Rock, former chair of the ACS Division of Chemical Education's Committee on Computers in Chemical Education, and a Fellow of the International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry (IUPAC). He is the IUPAC Task Group Chair for the InChI OER (International Chemical Identifier Open Education Resource), an OER on the use of the INCHI semantic identifier. He is also the project lead for Reaxys Education, an OER on Elsevier's Reaxys chemical information portal. Belford was lead-PI on the NSF funded Cheminformatics OLCC, an intercollegiate course taught on 14 campuses that was designed to introduce modern cheminformatics into the undergraduate chemistry curriculum, and all the content developed through the OLCC has been ported to the LibreText hyperlibrary. Belford is currently developing an Internet of Chemistry Things OER (IoCT OER), and after it has been properly vetted and curated, will transfer the IoCT OER content to the LibreText hyperlibrary. Belford uses LibreText in his general chemistry classes where he has created over 100 original videos, and has pioneered the use and integration of the Hypothes.is social annotation system into classroom activities. Through Hypothes.is, Belford's students can not only discuss any passage within his LibreTexts, but his students can highlight, annotate and directly link external web content to the relevant sections of his chapters, extending the discussions within his Libretext to content across the web. Belford's research interests deal with how cheminformatics is changing the fundamental cognitive artifacts used to represent, manipulate and communicate chemical information, and how these, combined with advanced social and semantic web informatics, can impact the practice of science and student learning

Dave Yaron
General Advisory Board member Professor Department of Chemistry Carnegie Mellon University
David Yaron is a Professor in the Department of Chemistry at Carnegie Mellon University with research interests in both computational chemistry and chemistry education. He has a Ph.D. in Chemical Physics from Harvard University and was a research associate at MIT before moving to Carnegie Mellon. His research in chemical education involves the development and assessment of activities for high school and introductory college chemistry. The virtual labs, tutorials, and scenario-based learning activities on his www.chemcollective.org site receive over 700,000 visitors each year, with over 450,000 virtual lab experiments being run. His Open Learning Initiative (OLI) online course materials cover a full year of introductory chemistry and provide students with activities that give hints and feedback as needed. He also recently co-chaired the College Board Committee that undertook a major redesign of the AP Chemistry course and exam. His research in computational chemistry involves development and use of quantum chemical methods for large systems, including especially organic materials for electronic and photophysical applications. Most recently, he has been working on ways to integrate machine learning into quantum chemical models and has developed the first neural network that performs quantum chemical calculations within the network.

Jason Lorgan
General Advisory Board member University of California, Davis
I have spent most of my career working in the course materials space at UC, CSU, CCC and private college campuses in California (UC Davis, San Diego State, Rio Hondo Community College and Golden Gate University). I have witnessed the equity issue created by students who can easily afford their course content and those who cannot. It has always puzzled me that most services that help create student success on college campuses are covered by tuition and fees with the exception of course materials. The cost of a course (tuition) is the same for any major, with the exception of the course material cost. Some majors are much more expensive than others when it comes to content costs. Once again, this is an equity issue for our students. As a Textbook Manager for many years, I have had many students crying or otherwise upset in my office explaining how difficult it can be to get through a course without being able to afford access to the content being used. These experiences have created my passion for supporting no and low cost content options for our students.

Interests: Snowboarding, Hiking, Motorcycle Riding, Weightlifting.
